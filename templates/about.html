The Story of Entify

"This project wasn't built to solve a specific commercial problem. Instead, it was created as a deep learning exercise to understand the internal mechanics of NLP and the architectural shift from classical models to neural networks."

Academic Context

ILM College

Sargodha Campus
Batch & Group BSCS (24-28) Group #2

The Teacher’s Challenge

Our journey began when Sir Abdur-Rehman, who teaches us Artificial Intelligence, announced a semester project focused on Natural Language Processing. He didn’t just give us a list of tasks. Instead, he told us to explore the field, find a problem worth solving, and then present a formal proposal as a group.

After we shared our plan to build a comparative system for Name Entity Recognition, he reviewed our project timeline & proposal. His feedback was incredibly encouraging, and with a simple "this is a good project," he officially approved our 8-week developmental cycle.

Strategic Evolution

Deciding on a Topic

It took us a lot of brainstorming to find the right focus. In the beginning, we actually explored several different paths, including building a fake news detector, a social media sentiment tool, and even image or audio-voice detectors. However, each of those had major drawbacks for an 8-week production sprint.

We realized that image and audio detection were moving too far away from pure Natural Language Processing. They would have required a whole set of extra libraries for computer vision and audio processing that weren't the core focus of our study. For fake news, we found that models are usually trained on fixed datasets.

"Even social media analysis was tricky because the necessary APIs from platforms like Facebook are often quite costly. We wanted a project that wasn't just a prototype but could actually be 'production-ready' by the time we finished."

Named Entity Recognition allowed exactly that. It has a stable, fixed dataset in CoNLL2003, allowing us to build a robust, professional system that functions perfectly on a live server.

Growing as a Team

The team dynamics were quite interesting during the first two weeks. While Hassan Ali already had experience as a developer, Mudassir and Saad Ilyas had to start from the ground up.

They spent those early days diving deep into Python OOP, mastering how to manage code with Git, and learning the fundamentals of Tkinter for desktop design. It was intensive, but it ensured everyone was ready when the real coding started.

The Last-Minute Pivot

Believe it or not, we actually finished a complete desktop version of this tool using Tkinter by the sixth week. It worked well and had a nice interface.

But since we were already studying Web Technologies at the time, we felt that a web platform would be much more impactful. We spent the final two weeks redesigning everything in Flask and deploying it on an AWS-EC2 server at {{ DEPLOY_DOMAIN }} to make it accessible to everyone.
