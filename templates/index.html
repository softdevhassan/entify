Dataset Case Study

The CoNLL2003 Paradox

While training on 14,041 news segments, we discovered why raw math isn't enough. Our custom CRF model often labeled "Google" as a Location (LOC) rather than an organization.

"...they were meeting at Google headquarters in Zurich."

Because "Google" frequently follows spatial prepositions like "at" or "in" in the 2003 Reuters dataset, the statistical model learned that "at [Word]" usually implies a physical location. This explains the specific performance gap we see when compared to neural networks.
