Dataset Case Study

The CoNLL2003 Paradox

While training on 14,041 news segments, we observed that the CRF model often struggles with context: labeling "Google" as LOC (Location) instead of ORG.

"...they were meeting at Google headquarters in Zurich."

Statistical models learn that words following "at" or "in" are typically physical locations, explaining the performance gaps rectified by modern neural networks.
